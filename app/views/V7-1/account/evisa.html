Prove your status

If you need to prove your immigration status to someone, you can do this online with a share code.


What you can do in the UK

As a skilled worker, you can work:

  • in your sponsored job
  • up to an extra 20 hours a week in another job. This role must be the same occupation code as your sponsored job or on the Shortage Occupation List. Your part time job must be outside the hours you work for your sponsor
  • as a volunteer. This is unpaid except for reasonsable expenses
  • the notice period for a job you were doing when you applied

You can also:

  • study as long as it does not interfere with the job you are sponsored for. This must be in line with the conditions listed in the application decision letter or email you received from UK Visas and Immigration
  • rent somewhere to live
  • use the NHS in a similar way to permanent UK residents
  • access a current account with a bank or building society in the UK
  • travel in and out of the country without having to prove your status, as your information will be checked automatically

You may be able to bring in your spouse or partner, as well as children aged under 18 who depend on you for care.

Things you cannot do

You cannot:

  • settle in the UK with this visa
  • get public funds

Register with the police

You must register with the police within 7 days of you:

  • arriving in the UK if you applied for a visa from outside the UK
  • being notified of your new status if you were already in the UK when you applied

Even if you have previously registered, you must do so again. You must take your decision letter or email with you. Visit UK visas and registering with the police for information on where to go, what other documents to take and how to report any change to your circumstances.

National Insurance number

You need a National Insurance number if you plan to work in the UK.

If you do not already have a National Insurance number and it has been more than 10 days since you got your visa, you need to apply for one. You can only apply from inside the UK.
