You need to have a UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) account to apply

Who has a UKVI account

You already have a UKVI account if you applied:

  • to the EU Settlement Scheme
  • for a visa and used the ‘UK Immigration: ID Check’ app to scan your identity document on your mobile phone

If you have an account already, sign in to start your application.

What you need to create an account

You will need:

  • your identity document
  • access to your email address
  • access to your mobile phone number

What you can use your UKVI account for

You can use your UKVI account to:

  • view and prove your rights to others – for example, your right to work for employers
  • keep your details up to date so you avoid unnecessary delays at the UK border
  • make other online immigration applications in the future

Giving someone access to help you with your online application

If you want someone to complete your application for you or help you with it, you can give them access to your account. You will need an email address and mobile phone number for them. They will need these to sign in to your account. You can remove their access at any time.

The Home Office will use the personal information you provide to create your account. Find out how the Home Office will process your personal information.


Sign in

To sign in you will need:

  • your identity document – your passport, national identity card, or biometric residence card or permit
  • access to your mobile phone or email address – you will get a security code to confirm your identity

Use the identity document, mobile phone number and email address you gave when when you created your account. If you have updated your UKVI account with new details since, use the new details.
